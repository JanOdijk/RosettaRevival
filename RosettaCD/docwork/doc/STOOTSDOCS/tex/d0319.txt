\section{Introduction}
This document discusses the subgrammar PREPPPROPformation for Dutch.

\section{Subgrammar Specification}
\begin{description}
  \item[Head] SUBPREP
  \item[Export] PREPPPROP
  \item[Import]  ADJPPROPVAR,ADVPPROPVAR, CLAUSEVAR, NPPROPVAR,
	        PREPPPROPVAR, CNVAR, NPVAR, PROSENTVAR, 
                PREPPVAR, ADVPVAR, SENTENCEVAR, EMPTYVAR, VERBPPROPVAR
  \item[file] dutch:rc\_ppsubgs.mrule (mrules81) (mrules81)
\end{description}

\section{Control Expression}


 The control expression can be represented in the following way:

\begin{verbatim}
 (RC\_startpreppproprules)
.(TC\_Preppatternrules)
.{RC_ppptempadvvar}
.{RC_pppsentadvvar}
.{RC_ppplocadvvar}
.(RC_pppvoice)
.[RC_pppmod]

\end{verbatim}

%The notation {\em []!} and {\em \{\}!} is used to indicate that there are
%filters associated to the rule or transformation class.

Transformation classes begin their names with {\em TC\_}, rule classes with
{\em  RC\_}. 

%Names without these prefixes stand for more complex control 
%expressions of rules and /or transformations naturally belonging together.
%These will be discussed below.

The real control expression used in the system does not use names of
rule and transformation classes, but only names of individual rules and
transformations.


\section{Rule and Transformation Classes}


\begin{mruleclass}{RC\_startpreppproprules}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind obligatory rules
\classtask to combine a SUBPREP and variables, if any, into a PREPPPROP
\end{classdescr}

\begin{members}
\begin{member}
\rulename Rstartpreppprop000
\ruletask to form PREPPPROPs headed by impersonal intransitive prepositions
\file dutch:rc\_ppsubgs.mrule (mrules81)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example
het aan; het uit; het in;  (\to het is aan, etc.)
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename Rstartpreppprop100
\ruletask  to form PREPPPROPs headed by personal intransitive prepositions
\file dutch:rc\_ppsubgs.mrule (mrules81)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example hij  aan/ uit/ op/ in (\to hij is aan / uit / op)
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename rstartpreppprop120
\ruletask  to form PREPPPROPs headed by transitive prepositions
\file dutch:rc\_ppsubgs.mrule (mrules81)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example in + x1 + x2 \to [x1 in x2]
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\end{members}

\nofilters

\nospeedrules

\noplannedrules

\norulesnotince

\begin{comments}
No rules have been made to deal with such PREPPs as {\em  van Amsterdam (via 
Utrecht) naar Groningen}. This has been done on purpose, because it was 
expercted that such rules would yield enormous ambiguities. Of course, they
still should be added.
\end{comments}

\end{mruleclass}

\begin{mruleclass}{RC\_ppptempadvvar}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind iterative rules
\classtask to introduce temporal adverbial variables
\end{classdescr}

\begin{members}
\begin{member}
\rulename Rppprefvarinsertion
\ruletask Introduction of variables for referential time adverbials that 
are not retrospective. These time adverbials are supposed to be of category 
ADVP, PREPP or SENTENCE. They will be substituted in the substitution rules.
\file dutch:rc\_ppsubgs.mrule (mrules81)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename rpppdurvarinsertion
\ruletask Introduction of variables for durative time adverbials.
These time adverbials are supposed to be of category 
ADVP, PREPP or SENTENCE. They will be substituted in the substitution rules.
(Perfective (i.e. inaccessible) adverbials combine with stative and activity 
Aktionsart; imperfective (i.e. accessible) adverbials combine with 
accomplishment and achievement Aktionsart, but this is not checked here but in 
the substitution rules for durative time adverbials).
\file dutch:rc\_ppsubgs.mrule (mrules81)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename Rpppretrovarinsertion
\ruletask Introduction of variables for referential time adverbials that 
are retrospective. These time adverbials are supposed to be of category 
ADVP, PREPP or SENTENCE. They will be substituted in the substitution rules.
A perfective adverbial can only be inserted into an imperfective clause.
\file dutch:rc\_ppsubgs.mrule (mrules81)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\end{members}
