$c1 Introduction
$ In the documents R0009, R0016, R0017, R0022 and R0028
 the Dutch grammar of Rosetta2 is described (except for 
the
morphological rules, which will be documented in the near future).
The purpose of this document is to supply some information about the basic 
lexicon, B-LEX. For 
complete information the reader will have to use his terminal
("u dutch"), here only a sample can be given. I will give the examples
without comments, I refer to R0009 for an explanation of the attributes.$
$ In B-LEX a distinction can be made between the "real" B-LEX containing
only basic expressions in the restricted sense (e.g. BADJs) 
and the extended B-LEX, containing
syncategorematic expressions (e.g. REFLPROs) as well. Here examples 
of both types of basic
expressions will be given.$
$ I will give examples of lexical entries for each lexical and/or 
basic syntactic category (except VAR and BUG), alphabetically ordered
according to category. In addition to the entry of B-LEX I specify the string
corresponding to this entry according to MDICT. For the 5 categories where
there is a distinction between the stem and the inflected form (BADJ, BNOUN,
BVERB, BQADJ, BPERSPRO), I will give in addition a
few examples of entries of inflected forms.$
$ If the number of words in a specific category is not too large, I 
I will list them completely.$
