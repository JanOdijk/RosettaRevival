\section{Introduction}

This document describes which subgrammars exist in Rosetta3, and how they
function in the overall grammar.
 
\section{Partial Isomorphic Subgrammars}

For every major category (VERB, ADJ, ADV, PREP, NOUN) 
a number of partially isomorphic 
subgrammars were planned to derive `full clauses' and to derive
`small clauses'. 

These subgrammars, plus an indication of what rules they consist of: 

\begin{description}
  \item[Xderivation] to deal with derivation.\footnote{X is a variable ranging 
over the major categories (VERB, ADJ, ADV, PREP, NOUN)} 
  \item[XPPROPformation] to form XPPROPs. This subgrammar deals with
argument structure, the syntactic realization of arguments, introduction of several 
variables for adverbials and voice.
  \item[XPPROPtoXPFORMULA] to form XPFORMULAs, the first step towards `small 
clauses'. It deals with tense and aspect, substitution of propositional 
phrases, EMPTY-substitution (a certain type of covert arguments).
  \item[XPPROPtoCLAUSE]  to form CLAUSEs, the first step towards `full clauses'.
It is partially isomorphic to XPPROPtoXPFORMULA and also deals with
tense and aspect, substitution of propositional 
phrases, EMPTY-substitution (a certain type of covert arguments).
   \item[XPFORMULAtoXPPROP] to form OPENXPPROPs and CLOSEDXPPROPs, the second
and last step to form small clauses. It deals with substitution of phrases 
(especially NPs), hence with scope, and with `mood'.
   \item[CLAUSEtoSENTENCE] to form SENTENCEs, the second and last step to
form `full clauses'. It is partially isomorphic to XPFORMULAtoXPPROP, hence
also deals with substitution of phrases 
(especially NPs), hence with scope, and with `mood'.
\end{description}

The derivation of a construction typically would run as follows:\\

\noindent
First, the Xderivation subgrammar is applied to BX to yield SUBX.\\

\noindent
Second, the XPPROPformation subgrammar is applied to SUBX to yield
an XPPROP.\\

\noindent
At this point a choice must be made: either a small clause is derived, or a 
full clause.\\ 

\noindent
If a small clause is derived the XPPROPtoXPFORMULA subgrammar
is applied to XPPROP to yield an XPFORMULA. Finally, the XPFORMULAtoXPPROP
subgrammar is applied to this XPFORMULA to yield an OPENXPPROP or a 
CLOSEDXPPROP.\\

\noindent
If a full clause is derived the XPPROPtoCLAUSE subgrammar is applied to
XPPROP to yield a CLAUSE. Finally, the CLAUSEtoSENTENCE subgrammar is applied
to this CLAUSE to yield a SENTENCE.
 
\noindent

The factual situation deviates slightly from this planning:

\begin{description}
  \item[XPPROPtoCLAUSE] According to the original planning there would be
five of such subgrammars: VERBPPROPtoCLAUSE, ADJPPROPtoCLAUSE, etc. It appeared
to be the case, however, that this would require an enormous repetition of 
identical rules. For this reason, there actually is only one subgrammar,
called XPPROPtoCLAUSE to deal with all of these cases.
  \item[NPPROPs] There is no isomorphy at all between the NPPROP subgrammars
and the other subgrammars. For NPPROPs the following subgrammars 
exist: Nounderivation, NPPROPformation, toNPPROP.
  \item[VERBPPROP] The subgrammars VERBPPROPtoVERBPformula and 
VERBPFormulatoVERBPPROP have not been made. It turned out to be much easier
to derive CLOSEDVERBPPROPs in the CLAUSEtoSENTENCE subgrammar.
\end{description}

These subgrammars are represented in the following table, where {\em PROP} has
been abbreviated to {\em P} and {\em FORMULA} to {\em F}:

\begin{tabular}[t]{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
     & --             & --                  & XPPtoCLAUSE & CLAUSEtoSENTENCE \\
\hline
VERB & VERBderivation & VERBPPformation & -- & -- \\
\hline
ADJ  & ADJderivation & ADJPPformation & ADJPPtoADJPF &  ADJPFtoADJPP \\
\hline
ADV  & ADVderivation & ADVPPformation & ADVPPtoADVPF & ADVPFtoADVPP \\
\hline
PREP & PREPderivation & PREPPformation & PREPPPtoPREPPF & PREPPFtoPREPPP\\
\hline
NOUN & NPderivation & NPPformation & -- & toNPP\\
\hline
\end{tabular}


The motivation to partition the subgrammars for a certain category in the ay
it has been done is as follows:

\begin{description} 
  \item[] The Xderivation subgrammars are separated from the XPPROPformation 
subgrammars, because the export of the Xderivation subgrammar must be input
for other Xderivation subgrammars. 
  \item[] The XPPROPformation subgrammars are separated from
the XPPROPtoCLAUSE and XPPROPtoXPFORMULA subgrammars to make the switch from
XPPROPformation subgrammar (for any X) to the XPPROPtoCLAUSE subgrammar
possible.
  \item[] The separation between the XPPROPtoCLAUSE and the CLAUSEtoSENTENCE 
subgrammars, and the separation between the XPPROPtoFORMULA and the 
XPFORMULAtoXPPROP subgrammars is motivated solely by the fact that one 
subgrammar for these cases would yield extreemly large subgrammars. It has no
principled status.
\end{description}

\section{Other Subgrammars}

Rosetta3 contains a number of other subgrammars. They can be partitioned
into the following classes:

\begin{description}
  \item[NP related subgrammars] These are the subgrammars 
{\bf CNFORMATION} and  {\bf NPFORMATION}, and the subgrammars
{\bf QPFormation, QPtoQPPROP,  DETDERIVATION} and
{\bf DETPFORMATION}. The subgrammar QPtoQPPROP is not operative yet. It is 
intended to deal with such sentences as {\em dit is genoeg}, but these can be 
derived in the NPPROP subgrammars as well.

  \item[Special Sentences] The subgrammars {\bf Existential} and {\bf 
Identificational} are used to form existential sentences and identificational
sentences, respectively. They form NPPROPs from NP, and by applying 
XPPROPtoCLAUSE and CLAUSEtoSENTENCE to these NPPROPs sentences are derived.

 \item[ADVP,PREPP] Apart from the subgrammars to derive OPENADVPPROPs and
CLOSEDADVPPROPs there are subgrammars to derive ADVPs and PREPPs. They are
used to derive temporal, locative and modal or sentential adverbial phrases.
These subgrammars, {\bf Preppformation} and {\bf AdvPformation}, are partially
isomorphic.

 \item[UTT] There is a special subgrammar to form utterances, called the
{\bf Utterance} subgrammar.
\end{description}

\section{List of Subgrammars}

The following list is a list of all subgrammars for Dutch in Rosetta3, ordered 
alphabetically. For each grammar the {\em head} and the {\em export} categories
are specified (in the format {\em head} \to {\em export}). The file in which
the subgrammar can be found is specified as well.


\begin{description}
   \item[ADJDERIVATION ] BADJ \to SUBADJ  (mrules48.mrule)
   \item[ADJPFORMULAtoADJPPROP ] ADJPFORMULA \to (OPENADJPPROP, CLOSEDADJPPROP)
                            (mrules48.mrule)
   \item[AdjppropFormation ] SUBADJ \to ADJPPROP  (mrules48.mrule)
   \item[ADJPPROPtoADJPFORMULA ] ADJPPROP \to  ADJPFORMULA (mrules48.mrule)
   \item[Advderivation ] (BADV,RADV) \to SUBADV  (mrules95.mrule)
   \item[AdvPformation  ] SUBADV \to ADVP  (mrules95.mrule)
   \item[ADVPformulatoADVPprop  ] ADVPFORMULA \to (OPENADVPPROP, 
CLOSEDADVPPROP)    (mrules95.mrule)
   \item[Advppropformation  ] SUBADV \to ADVPPROP (mrules95.mrule)
   \item[ADvpproptoADVPformula  ] ADVPPROP \to ADVPFORMULA (mrules95.mrule)
   \item[CLAUSEtoSENTENCE ] CLAUSE \to (SENTENCE, CLOSEDVERBPPROP) (mrules3.mrule)
   \item[CNFORMATION ] (SUBNOUN, EN) \to CN  (mrules67.mrule)
   \item[DETDERIVATION ] BDET \to DET  (mrules67.mrule)
   \item[DETPFORMATION ] (QP, NUM, DET) \to DETP  (mrules67.mrule)
   \item[Existential ] NP \to NPPROP  (mrules58.mrule)
   \item[Identificational ] NP \to NPPROP  (mrules59.mrule)
   \item[NPDERIVATION ] (BNOUN , BPROPERNOUN, BINDEFPRO, BPERSPRO, BWHPRO, 
BBIGPRO) \to (SUBNOUN, PROPERNOUN, INDEFPRO, PERSPRO, BIGPRO) 
PROPERNOUN, INDEFPRO)  (mrules67.mrule)
   \item[NPFORMATION ] (CN, INDEFPRO, PROPERNOUN, WHPRO, DEMPRO, BIGPRO, 
RECIPRO, PERSPRO) \to NP   (mrules67.mrule)
   \item[NPPROPformation ] NP \to NPPROP (mrules67.mrule)
   \item[Prepderivation ] PREP \to  SUBPREP (mrules81.mrule)
   \item[Preppformation ] SUBPREP \to  PREPP (mrules91.mrule)
   \item[Preppformulatopreppprop ] PREPPFORMULA \to (OPENPREPPPROP,
CLOSEDPREPPPROP)    (mrules83.mrule)
   \item[Prepppropformation ] SUBPREP \to PREPPPROP (mrules81.mrule)
   \item[Preppproptopreppformula ] PREPPPROP \to PREPPFORMULA  (mrules82.mrule)
   \item[QPFormation ] Q \to QP (mrules45.mrule)
   \item[QPtoQPPROP] QP \to QPPROP  (mrules45.mrule) (not operative).
   \item[toNPPROP ] NPPROP \to (OPENNPPROP, CLOSEDNPPROP)  (mrules67.mrule)
   \item[XPPROPtoCLAUSE ] (VERBPPROP, ADJPPROP, NPPROP, ADVPPROP, PREPPPROP) 
\to CLAUSE  (mrules9.mrule)
   \item[Utterance ] (NP, PREPP, ADVP, CLOSEDADVPPROP, OPENADJPPROP, SENTENCE) 
\to  UTT (mrules1.mrule)
   \item[Verbderivation ] BVERB \to SUBVERB  (mrules60.mrule)
   \item[Verbppropformation ] SUBVERB \to VERBPPROP  (mrules28.mrule)
\end{description}
