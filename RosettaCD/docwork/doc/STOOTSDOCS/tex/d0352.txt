\section{Introduction}
In this document the subgrammar ADJPPROPformation for Spanish is described.
The parts on temporal adverbs and aktionsart calculation have been written by 
Lisette Appelo, the other parts have been written by Franciska de Jong
and Elly 
van Munster.


This grammar is supposed to be isomorphic to the 
subgrammar VERBPPROPformation. It is relevant to {\bf all} 
adjectival constructions, 
whether they end up as ADJP-utterance, as NP-internal constituent, or as
clausal constituent.


\section{Subgrammar Specification}
\begin{description}
  \item[Head] SUBADJ
  \item[Export] ADJPPROP
  \item[Import] SUBADJ, EMPTYVAR, CNVAR, NPVAR, SENTENCEVAR, PREPP, PREPPVAR,
ADVP, ADVPVAR, QP, NP, SENTENCE
                
  \item[File] spanish:ADJsubgrammars.mrule (mrules78.mrule)
\end{description}

\section{Control Expression}
The control expression can be defined as follows:
\begin{verbatim}
(RSTARTADJPPROP000/1 | RSTARTADJPPROP100/2 | RSTARTADJPPROP120/3 )

. (TADJPATTERN0/7 |  TADJPATTERN12a/10 | TADJPATTERN12b/11 
  | TADJPATTERN14/14 | TADJPATTERN16/17 )

. [RADJPARAOBJMOD/25]

. [RADJMOD1/26 ]

. [RADJDEGREEMOD1/28 | RADJDEGREEMOD2a/29 | RADJDEGREEMOD2b/30 ] 

. {RADJRefVARinsertion/101 | RADJDurVARinsertion/102 
  | RADJRetroVARinsertion/103 }

. {Radjcauspreppvar/31 | Radjppconjsvar/50}

. { Radjlocpreppvar/33 }

. RADJVOICEdefault/35 
 
. TADJAktStative1/104 

. TC_ADJreflspelling

\end{verbatim}
For testing purposes some of the rules are not activated in the present 
system.

\begin{mruleclass}{TC\_ADJpatternrules}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind obligatory transformation class
\classtask spelling out the adjpatterns
\classremarks
The distinction between voorobjs and prepobjs that in Dutch 
requires the distinction between {\em synPREPNP}
 (TADJPATTERN12a) and {\em synVOORNP}
TADJPATTERN18a does not exist. All adjectives that take a non-sentential 
complement have {\em synPREPNP} as a value for .adjpatterns.
In sentences such as {\em Ella es importante para el proyecto} the 
{\em para}-PREPP is presumed to be a modifier (introduced by RADJPARAOBJMOD),
 while the value for .thetaadj is adjp100 (not:adjp120).
As a consequence, this TC has no counterpart for the Dutch rules 
TADJPATTERN18a. The motivation for distinguishing  
synVOOREMPTY (TADJPATTERN18b )
and synPREPEMPTY (TADJPATTERN14) for Dutch 
pertains to the semantics of the EMPTYVARs.
The substitution of the EMPTYVAR 
introduced by TADJpattern18b is supposed not to 
amount to Existential Quantification, as 
is the case with TADJpattern14.
As for Spanish no relevant examples have been found yet, the Dutch
TADJPATTERN18b has no Spanish counterpart. (Actually there are no clear 
Dutch examples any more either, 
since the cases that led to the introduction of 
synVOOREMPTY are presnetly  treated as modifiers.)
\nofilters

\nospeedrules

\noplannedrules

\norulesnotince

\rulelist
\end{classdescr}

\remarks\mbox{}
translational problem: as translation of the Dutch adjs with 
synOPENINFSENTPROOBJ, Spanish  uses 
constructions:
\begin{enumerate}
\item  middle-construction: este cucillo corta bien (voor: dit mes is 
handig om mee PRO te snijden)
\item  a "generic" construction paralle to {\em in dit boek schrijf je niet} (
dit boek is niet om in PRO te schrijven)
\item  infinitival  {\em para}-construction without pro-object counterpart:
este cucillo es para cortar (queso)  (dit mes is MEANT om (kaas) 
mee PRO te snijden
\end{enumerate}

\end{member}

\begin{mruleclass}{RCS\_ADJparaobjmod}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind optional rule class
\classtask introduction of a {\em para}-modifier
\classremarks 
The modifiers introduced by means of this RC are quite argument-like.
The reason that they are not treated as real arguments and hence not introduced
by RC\_startadjpprop/TC\_Adjpattern is that they are always optional and that 
they do not behave as prepobjs. It {\em does} allow local translation.
\nofilters

\nospeedrules

\noplannedrules

\norulesnotince

\rulelist
\end{classdescr}

\begin{members}
\begin{member}
\rulename RADJPARAOBJMOD
\ruletask introduction of a {\em para-}modifier to adjectives with the value 
{\em subjectiveadj} in .subcs.
\file spanish:RCS\_ADJmod.mrule (mrules61.mrule)
\semantics modification
\example es facile para las(!) mujeres
\remarks\mbox{}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item 

Probably it is possible to define severe dictionary constraints
on the set of adjpatterns for adjectives with .sucbs = subjectiveadj.
  \item
If the complement of preposition {\em para} is 
the translation of a bare plural it is generic and hence 
requires a definite article in Spanish
\end{enumerate}

\end{member}
\end{members}
\end{mruleclass}
\begin{mruleclass}{RCS\_ADJmod}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind optional rule class
\classtask  introduction of modifiers to adjectives other than degree- 
modifiers and {\em para}-modifiers
\classremarks
In this RC RADJparainfmod1 was originally planned as a counterpart for the dutch
RADJOMTEMOD1. However, constructions such as
?{\em (hermoso) para ver} or ?{\em (c\'{o}modo) para llevar}
seem not to be correct. 

The content of the dutch examples such as (i) and (ii)
that are dealt with
by RADJOMTEMOD1, 
can be  translated only 
into Spanish structures that do not involve modification, namely the structures that
correspond to the dutch examples (iii) and (iv) 
(dummy {\em het} and extraposed sentential 
subject argument).

(i) zij is leuk om te ontmoeten \\ 
(ii) dit is handig om mee te nemen\\ 
(iiiDU) het is leuk om haar te ontmoeten\\
(iiiSP) es agradable recontrar la\\
(ivDU) het is handig om dit mee te nemen\\
(ivSP) es c\'{o}modo llevar se lo; es pr\'{a}tico de llevar\\

This could be accounted for straightforwardly if the two  constructions
of dutch were related (that is, if parallel derivations would exist). 
Whether they can and should be related is still to be investigated. 
At least the following questions need to be answered: 
\begin{enumerate}
\item
Do the two constructions 
have identical meanings? (Note that {\em leuk om te zien} is perhaps an 
idiomatic structure with a {\em leuk} that is not identical to the 
{\em leuk} the construction with the dummy {\em het}; in Spanish it 
translates into 
{\em attractiva}.) 
\item
Do the struture with non-dummy subject really involve modification?
Note that from {\em x1 is ADJ om ....} does not always impl {\em x1 is ADJ}.

\end{enumerate}
\nofilters

\nospeedrules

\noplannedrules

\norulesnotince

\rulelist
\end{classdescr}

\begin{members}
\begin{member}
\rulename RADJmod1
\ruletask  introduction of modifiers such as {\em (sterk) als een leeuw}, 
\file spanish:RCS\_ADJmod.mrule (mrules61.mrule)
\semantics modification
\example fuerte como un l\'{e}on,  amarillento .. como si ...
\remarks\mbox{}
Alternative: idiom-treatment. 

\end{member}
\end{members}
\end{mruleclass}
