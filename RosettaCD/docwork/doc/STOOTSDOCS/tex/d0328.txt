\section{Introduction}
This document discusses the subgrammar CLAUSEtoSENTENCE for Spanish.\\
The CLAUSE grammar of Spanish is divided into the following parts:\\
\begin{itemize}
\item VERBderivation (see doc:r0349)
\item VERBPPROPformation (see doc:r0301)
\item XPPROPtoCLAUSE (see doc:r0334)
\item CLAUSEtoSENTENCE
\item UTTERANCE (see doc:r0344)
\end{itemize}
This document discusses the subgrammar CLAUSEtoSENTENCE for Spanish.
In section 2 a specification of the subgrammar is given (i.e. the Head,
Import and Export Categories). \\
In section 3 the rules and transformation are listed in the so-called
Control Expression.\\
In section 4 documentation on the rules and transformations is given.
Each subsection contains a rule class or transformation class.\\
In principle, the rules/transformations have been documented by their
own author: Jan Odijk wrote the sections on TC\_WHshift, RC\_NegVarIntro,
RC\_substitution, RC\_mooddetermination, RC\_conjsent and TC\_inversion. 
Lisette Appelo wrote the sections on TC\_FC\_TempEmptyDeletion,
TC\_FC\_DeixisRetroAdaptation and
TC\_FC\_finaltransformations.\\
The other sections have been written by Elly van Munster.


\section{Subgrammar Specification}
\begin{description}
  \item[Head] CLAUSE
  \item[Export] SENTENCE
  \item[Import] NP, ADVP, PREPP, PROSENT, POS, NEG, CONJ, PREP

  \item[File] spanish:ClausetoSentence.mrule (mrules27.mrule)
\end{description}

\begin{mruleclass}{TC\_WHshift}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind Obligatory Transformation Class.
\classtask to shift constituents if required, i.e. if they have mood =
wh, relativexpmood. It should be extended to deal with topicalization.
\end{classdescr}

The following shift rules exist:
\begin{description}
  \item[Twhshift1a] for shifting inside a sentence 
  \item[Twhshift1b] for shifting inside a sentence 
  \item[Twhshift0] to let structures where shifting should not apply pass
the transformation class
  \item[Twhshift2] for shifting out of a subordinate clause
\end{description}


\begin{members}
\begin{member}
\rulename Twhshift0
\ruletask To let structures where shifting does not apply pass the 
transformation class.
\file spanish:TC\_WHshift.mrule (mrules84.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example Juan compra un libro \to Juan compra un libro.
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename TWhshift1a
\ruletask For the actual shifting, within a sentence, for phrases under CLAUSE.
\file spanish:TC\_WHshift.mrule (mrules84.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example   For every subrule an example is supplied:  
\begin{description}
    \item[subjrel] ?`Qui\'{e}n le ha visto?
    \item[tempadvrel/ADVPVAR] ?`Cu\'{a}ndo compr\'{o} el libro?
    \item[locadvrel/ADVPVAR] ?`D\'{o}nde compr\'{o} el libro?
    \item[tempadvrel/PREPPVAR] ?`En qu\'{e} momento compr\'{o} el libro?
    \item[locadvrel/PREPPVAR] ?`En qu\'{e} ciudad compr\'{o} el libro?
\end{description}
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename Twhshift1b
\ruletask For the actual shifting, within a sentence, for phrases under VERBP.
\file spanish:TC\_WHshift.mrule (mrules84.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example For every subrule an example is supplied:
\begin{description}
    \item[objrel] ?`Qu\'{e} ha comprado?
    \item[indobjrel] ?`A qui\'{e}n ha dado un libro?
    \item[paraobjrel] ?`Para qui\'{e}n ha comprado un libro?
    \item[porobjrel] ?`Por qui\'{e}n ha venido?
    \item[predrel/ADJP] ?`C\'{o}mo de grande se ha hecho?
    \item[predrel/NP] ?`Qu\'{e} sali\'{o}?\\
     (Sali\'{o} buen m\'{u}sico = Hij is een goed musicus geworden).
    \item[predrel/NPVAR] ?`Qu\'{e} es eso?
    \item[locargrel/VARPREPP] ?`D\'{o}nde est\'{a}?
    \item[dirargrel/VARPREPP]  ?`D\'{o}nde se sent\'{o}? \\
    (= Waar is hij gaan zitten?)
    \item[prepobjrel/VARPREPP] ?`En qu\'{e} insiste?
\end{description}
\remarks The following remarks  can be made:
\begin{description}
  \item[predrel] We allow for movemen of predrel?ADJP and predrel/NP despite
the fact that they are not variables. Of course, relativization and 
topicalization of such constituents cannot take place. 
  \item[vpadvrel] Certain constituents cannot be shifted yet, e.g. 
{\em vpadvrels}. The rules should be extended to deal with them. Notice,
 however, that there is no variable for such adverbials.
  \item[topicalization] The rules should be extended to deal with 
topicalization.
\end{description}


\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename Twhshift2
\ruletask For shifting out of a subordinate clause.
\file spanish:TC\_WHshift.mrule (mrules84.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example ?`D\'{o}nde dijo que hab\'{\i}a comprado aquel libro?
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\end{members}

\begin{mruleclass}{RC\_Substitution}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind Iterative Rule Class
\classtask Substitution of different categories for their corresponding
variables in various configurations.\\
The following substitutionrules exist.
\begin{description}
\item[ 0.] NP in a VP-complsent in a VP (idioms) (RIDSubstitution1)
\item[ 1.] NP in S , nongeneric (Rsubstitution1) (subjrel, shiftrel)
\item[ 2.] NP in VP, nongeneric (Rsubstitution2) ( idrel,objrel, predrel)
\item[ 3.] NP in VARPREPP in VP, nongeneric (Rsubstitution3)
            (prepobjrel, locargrel, dirargrel, instradvrel, 
              porobjrel, indobjrel, paraobjrel)
\item[ 4.] (VAR)PREPP for  PREPPVAR in S , nongeneric (Rsubstitution4)
            ( locadvrel, causadvrel, shiftrel)
\item[ 5.] ADVP under S (Rsubstitution5), (locadvrel)
\item[ 7.] PROSENT (loPROSENT) in the VP (Rsubstitution7)
\item[ 8.] POS in S (Rsubstitution8)
\item[ 9.] NEG in S (Rsubstitution*)
\item[10.] NP in VARPREPP in S (shiftrel) (Rsubstitution10)
\item[11.] temporal ADVP (Rtempadvsubstitution1)
\item[12.] temporal PREP (Rtempadvsubstitution2)
\end{description}

The following extensions are to be made:

\begin{description}
  \item[4a] There should be a rule to substitute (VAR)PREPPs for PREPPVARs
under VP, for {\em  vpadvrels, instradvrels}. Cf. Dutch rsubstitution4b.
  \item[5.] Rule Rsubstitution5 should be extended to deal with {\em  
causadvrel} and {\em  shiftrel}
   \item[ 6.] There should be a rule to substitute 
sentadvrel/.. (Rsubstitution6), cf. Dutch  Rsubstitution9.
  \item[ADJP] Rules to substitute into ADJPs should be added 
(cf. Dutch Rsubstitution3a,Rsadjsubstitution1).
  \item[BIGPRO] There should be a rule to substitute BIGPRO-NPs, cf. Dutch
Rbigprosubst
\end{description}


\end{classdescr}

\begin{members}
\begin{member}
\rulename RIDSubstitution1
\ruletask Substitution of NP for NPVAR in a VERBP-complsent in a VERBP.
\file spanish:RC\_substitution.mrule (mrules24.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example x1 mandar a x2  a x2 buscar x3
\remarks This rule is quite problematic. It substitutes -so to speak-
too deep, so that all sorts of transformations (a-insertion, a+el glue-ing, 
etc.) should be repeated to get correct results.
\end{member}

\begin{member}
\rulename Rsubstitution1
\ruletask Substitution of NP for NPVAR to the left of the VERBP 
(subjrel, shiftrel).
\file spanish:RC\_substitution.mrule (mrules24.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example
x1 nada $\rightarrow$ Juan nada\\
x1 ha visto \to Qu\'{e} ha visto
\remarks
\begin{itemize}
\item In COMP the attribute value "generic" is set to "omegageneric" for
efficiency reasons: an NP is not generic by itself, but that is 
determined by the
context. So at the surface (and thus for the surface-parser) the value can be 
omega.
\item A QUOTE function to handle the supertiempo assignment should substitute 
the conditions and actions. There is an asymmetry in the rule having to do with
supertiempo.
\end{itemize}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename RSubstitution2

\begin{mruleclass}{TC\_FC\_Cliticrules}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind Optional Transformation Class plus Filters.
\classtask To create a corresponding DO-clitic when the DO is a PERSPRO.
The PERSPRO can be either deleted or not.\\
Furthermore, in some cases the IO-clitic has to be changed into another form
when a DO-clitic is added (see *Cambio* rules).
\end{classdescr}

\begin{members}
\begin{member}
\rulename TDOclplusPERSPRO
\ruletask To create a corresponding DO-clitic when the DO is a PERSPRO
(with Prepositive case, i.e. where an "a" is inserted later);
the PERSPRO is not deleted.
\file spanish:tc\_cliticrules.mrule (mrules87.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example veo (a) ella $\rightarrow$ la veo (a) ella\\
         saluda (a) ellos $\rightarrow$ los/les saluda (a) ellos
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename TDOclplusPERSPRO2
\ruletask To create a corresponding DO-clitic when the DO is a PERSPRO
(with Prepositive case, i.e. where an "a" is inserted later);
the PERSPRO is not deleted.\\ 
For infinite clauses. Gluerules follow.
\file spanish:tc\_cliticrules.mrule (mrules87.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example ver (a) ella $\rightarrow$ ver la (a) ella\\
saludar (a) ellos $\rightarrow$  saludar los/les (a) ellos
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}
\begin{member}
\rulename TDOclminusPERSPRO2
\ruletask To create a corresponding DO-clitic when the DO is a PERSPRO;
the PERSPRO is deleted. The DO can have either Prepositive case (i.e. "a"
would have been inserted later) or Accusative case. For infinite clauses; glue 
rules follow.
\file spanish:TC\_cliticrules2.mrule (mrules90.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example ver ella $\rightarrow$  ver la\\
saludar ellos $\rightarrow$  saludar los/les\\
comprar \'{e}l $\rightarrow$  comprar lo
\remarks\mbox{}
\item {\bf WARNING:} this rule is not reversible; analytically the NP-node is 
not recovered!

\item comment1:\\
This transformation is applicable for both Prepositive case (for which also
TDOclplusPERSPRO is applicable, i.e. the DO-PERSPRO can be either deleted or 
not) and for Accusative case (for which only this
transformation is applicable, i.e. the DO-PERSPRO h\'{a}s to be deleted).\\ \\
\item comment2:\\
No specification of mu4 and mu6 and mu10 is given yet. 
This will be necessary if an analytical component for Spanish is added.

\end{member}
\begin{member}

the PERSPRO is deleted. The DO can have either Prepositive case (i.e. "a"
would have been inserted later) or Accusative case.
\file spanish:TC\_cliticrules2.mrule (mrules90.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics

saluda ellos $\rightarrow$ los/les saluda\\

\begin{mruleclass}{RC\_conjsent}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind Optional Rule Class
\classtask to form adverbial sentences
\end{classdescr}

There are four rules to form adverbial subordinate clauses:
\begin{description}
 \item[Rconjsent1] finite subordinate adverbial clauses introduced by a
                     conjunction: cuando ven\'{\i}a
 \item[Rconjsent2] infinite subordinate adverbial clauses intorduced by a
                     conjunction: ?antes de venir
 \item[Rconjsent3] finite subordinate adverbial clauses intorduced by a
                     preposition: antes de que ven\'{i}a, despues de que..
 \item[Rconjsent4] infinite subordinate adverbial clauses intorduced by a
                     preposition:sin venir, antes de venir
\end{description}

The following rules might be added: a rule to form infinitival adverbial 
sentence with overt subject, e.g. {\em  por estar Juan enfermo}(because
Juan is ill); a rule
to form a gerundio adverbial sentence with overt subject, e.g. 
{\em  a\'{u}n estando Juan enfermo} (though Juan is ill).

Als conjuctionless gerundio-clauses might be dealt with here, by 
assuming an abstract CONJ that is multiply ambiguous and that is introduced
and immediately deleted in these rules.

\file spanish:RC\_conjsent.mrule (mrules101.mrule)
\semantics \nosemantics
\example
que vino + cuando $\rightarrow$ cuando vino\\
que llueve + porque $\rightarrow$ porque llueve
\remarks\mbox{}

\end{member}

\begin{mruleclass}{TC\_Inversion}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind Obligatory Transformation Class
\classtask To perform inversion
\end{classdescr}

The following scheme indicate which transformation should apply in which
situation.
Finite Sentences:\\

\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c||c|c|c|c|}
\hline
mood     & \multicolumn{4}{c||}{MAIN CLAUSE}& \multicolumn{4}{c|}{SUBORDINATE}\\
\hline
         & ++ & +-  & -+ & --& ++ & +- & -+ & --\\
\hline
wh       & 1  & 3   & *  & * & 1  & 3  & *  & *\\
decl     & *  & *   & 4  & 3 & 1  & 3  & 4  & 3\\
relative & *  & *   & *  & * & 1  & 3  & *  & *\\
whmod    & *  & *   & *  & * & 1  & 3  & *  & *\\
imper.   & *  & *   & 2  & 3 & *  & *  & *  & *\\
yesno    & *  & *   & 1  & 3 & *  & *  & 4  & 3\\
\hline
\end{tabular}

Infinite sentences: 4\\

Meaning of + and -, * and numbers:\\

\begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
\hline
++ & sentence contains a wh-shiftrel and a subjrel\\
+- & sentence contains a wh-shiftrel but no subjrel\\
-+ & sentence contains no  wh-shiftrel but it does contain a subjrel\\
-- & sentence contains no wh-shiftrel nor a subjrel\\
\mbox{}*  & this situation cannot occur\\
1  & the configuration is accounted for by tinversion1\\
2  & the configuration is accounted for by tinversion2\\
3  & the configuration is accounted for by tinversion3 (no inversion)\\
4  & the configuration is accounted for by tinversion4 (no inversion)\\
\hline
\end{tabular}


All cases have been tested, except for {\em  whmod}-cases. The following
sentences can be used for testing.
\begin{description}

\item[WH] \mbox{}\\
\begin{description} 
\item[wh++M] - Wat ziet de man $\rightarrow$ \\
- ?`Qu\'{e} ve el hombre? (Inversion1, wh, Main)
\item[wh+-M] - Wat ziet hij? $\rightarrow$ \\
- ?`Qu\'{e} ve? (Inversion3, wh, Main)
\item[wh++S] Ik weet wat de man ziet $\rightarrow$\\
- S\'{e} qu\'{e} ve el hombre (Inversion1, wh, Subordinate)
\item[wh+-S] Ik weet wat hij ziet $\rightarrow$\\
- S\'{e} qu\'{e} ve (Inversion3, wh, Subordinate)
\end{description}

\item[DECL] \mbox{}\\
\begin{description}
\item[decl-+M] - De man zwemt $\rightarrow$ \\
- El hombre nada (Inversion4, Decl, Main)
\item[decl--M]  Hij zwemt $\rightarrow$ \\
- Nada (Inversion3, Decl, Main)
\item[decl++S] - Wat denkt hij dat de man ziet? $\rightarrow$ \\
- ?`Q\'{e} cree que ve el hombre? (Inversion1, wh, Main)
\item[decl+-S] Wat denkt hij dat hij ziet? $\rightarrow$\\
- ?`Qu\'{e} cree que ve? (Inversion3, Decl, Subordinate)
\item[decl-+S] Hij denkt dat de man zwemt $\rightarrow$\\
- Cree que el hombre nada (Inversion4, Decl, Subordinate)
\item[decl--S] - Hij denkt dat hij zwemt $\rightarrow$ \\
- Cree que nada (Inversion3, Decl, Subordinate)
\end{description}

\item[RELATIVE] \mbox{}\\
\begin{description}
\item[relative++S] - de mannen die Jan ziet $\rightarrow$\\
- los hombres que ve Juan (Inversion1, Relative, Subordinate)
\item[relative+-S] de mannen die hij ziet $\rightarrow$\\
- los hombres que ve (Inversion3, Relative, Subordinate)  
\end{description}

\item[IMPER]\mbox{}\\
\begin{description}
\item[imp-+M] - Zwemt! $\rightarrow$ \\
- !`Nade usted! / !`Naden ustedes! (Inversion2, Imper, Main)
\item[imp--M] Zwem! $\rightarrow$ \\
- !`Nada! (Inversion3, Imper, Main)
\end{description}

\item[YESNO] \mbox{}\\
\begin{description}
\item[yesno-+M] - Zwemt de man? $\rightarrow$ \\
- ?`Nada el hombre? (Inversion1, yesno, Main)
\item[yesno-+M] - Ziet de man de os $\rightarrow$ \\
- ?`Ve el hombre el buey? (Inversion1, yesno, Main)
\item[yesno--M] - Zwemt hij? $\rightarrow$\\
- ?`Nada? (Inversion3, yesno, Main)
\item[yesno-+S] Ik vraag of de man zwemt $\rightarrow$\\
- Pregunto si el hombre nada (Inversion3, yesno, Subordinate) 
\item[yesno--S] Ik vraag of hij zwemt $\rightarrow$\\
- Pregunto si nada (Inversion3, yesno, Subordinate)
\end{description}

\end{description}

\begin{mruleclass}{RC\_Punctuation}
\begin{classdescr}
\kind Obligatory Rule Class
\classtask Making the punctuation in the sentence.\\
There are 4 punctuationrules:
\begin{description}
  \item[Rpunc0] for subordinate clauses; it makes subordinate clauses pass
                this rule class.
  \item[Rpunc1] places a `?`' at the beginning of main clause questions
                and a `?`' at the end.
  \item[Rpunc2] places a `!`' at the beginning of imperatives
                and a `!`' at the end.
  \item[Rpunc3] places a `.' after all other main clauses.
\end{description}
Since only Spanish generation is considered at this moment,
the absence of punctuation marks is not allowed yet in analysis.
(i.e. the decomp-rules have been made, but symmetrical to the comp-rules).



\end{classdescr}

                this rule class.

\remarks\mbox{}

                and a `?`' at the end.
