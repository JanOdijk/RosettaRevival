$C1 Introduction.
This document is a specification of the external behaviour of the Rosetta3 
system. We confine ourselves, however, to the system behaviour with respect
to the lay user. 
$NL
The user-interface of Rosetta3 will be specified by means of screen-layout
descriptions.
$NL
In chapter 2 the use of function keys is introduced. Chapter 3 describes
how to start a session with the Rosetta3 system. Finally in chapter 4 and 5
the translation phase of the system in different modes is specified.
$C1 Function keys.
A number of function keys of the VT220-keyboard will be used 
by the Rosetta3 system for input purposes. I will give here a
layout of the keyboard with respect to the function keys. 
$NL
The "HELP" function key may be pressed whenever the system allows a
function key to be entered. The "HELP" key provides extra information
in each system mode. Another function key that needs to be explained at this
point is the "RESET" key. Whenever it is allowed to press a "HELP" key it is 
also allowed to press the "RESET" key. Pressing the "RESET" key will bring
the system back to the initial state, just before the point where the user
is asked to choose a communication language ( see Chapter 3 ).
$NL
The other functions
corresponding with the function keys will be explained in the following
chapters. 
$NL 1
$UM
$RL 8
Group I (function keys F6-F10)

              .      .      .      .      .      .

                RESET  STOP   NEW    NEXT   NEXT   
                                     SENSE
              .      .      .      .      .      .

$RL 8

              .      .      .      .      .      

                WORD  PHRASE   INT    NO

$QM
$C1 Starting a Rosetta3 session.
Before the system startup can be performed, the "POWER ON" switch of the
VT220 has to be pressed. After typing a carriage return the text
$UM
 .                                                                 .
 
     Username:

 .                                                                 .

$QM
appears on the terminal screen. Now, the user has to type "ROSETTA3"
followed by a carriage return:
$UM
$RL 6
 .                                                                 .

     Username: ROSETTA3<ret>

 .                                                                 .

$QM
The screen clears and the following question appears at the upper left corner 
of the screen:
$UM
$RL 14
 .                                                                 .

    Kies de communicatie taal van het systeem door
    op een van de volgende functie-toetsen te drukken.

    Please, choose the communication language of the system by
    pressing one of the following function keys.

    Por favor, escoja el linguaje de comunicacio@'n del sistema
    empujando una de las siguientes teclas.

    <DUTCH>  : Nederlands, Dutch,     holande@'s
    <ENG>    : Engels,     English,   ingle@'s
    <SPAN>   : Spaans,     Spanish,   espan@~ol


 .                                                                 .

$QM
The user must now choose the language which will be used by the system
to communicate with him. There are three possible choices, Dutch,
English or Spanish. Suppose that the user chooses for Dutch by typing <DUTCH>.
( In the remaining part of the document we will use the communication 
language Dutch )
$NL
Next, the screen clears again and the following question appears.
$UM
$RL 6
 .                                                                 .

    Kies een brontaal, a.u.b.:

    Nederlands ( functie-toets <DUTCH> )
    Engels     ( functie-toets <ENG> )

    Druk op de corresponderende functie-toets.

 .                                                                 .

$QM
The user can make a choice for a source language in the translation process.
He can choose between Dutch ( <DUTCH> ), and English ( <ENG> ). Let
us assume that again his choice is Dutch.
$NL 1
Now another question appears on the screen:
$UM
$RL 7
 .                                                                 .

    Kies een doeltaal, a.u.b. : 

    Nederlands ( functie-toets <DUTCH> )
    Engels     ( functie-toets <ENG> )
    Spaans     ( functie-toets <SPA> )

    Druk op de corresponderende functie-toets.

 .                                                                 .

$QM
The user can make a choice for a target language from Dutch, English and
Spanish. Let us assume the user chooses English.
$NL 1
After answering this question the user will be asked to make a choice for
one of the two available system modes: word-level translation or
phrase-level translation.
$UM
$RL 10
 .                                                                 .

   Kies de methode van vertalen, a.u.b :

      - woord-niveau vertaling   ( functie-toets  <WORD>)
      - frase-niveau vertaling   ( functie-toets  <PHRASE>)

   Druk op de corresponderende functie-toets.

 .                                                                 .

$QM
Again the user can make his choice by pressing the corresponding
function key. Chapter 4 describes
what happens if the user presses the <WORD> function key. The consequences
of pressing <PHRASE> are shown in chapter 5. 
$NL
Only in the case that the user has chosen communication language Dutch and
_also_ source language Dutch the system will ask the following question:
$UM
$RL 10
 .                                                                 .

   Wilt u wel of niet de interactieve systeem-mode:

      - wel interactie           ( functie-toets  <INT>)
      - geen interactie          ( functie-toets  <NON-INT>)

   Druk op de corresponderende functie-toets.

 .                                                                 .

$QM
The interactive mode will only be possible in case of the Dutch (communication
language) Dutch (source language) choice.
Answering this
question completes the Rosetta3 start-up phase, the system will continue
now with the translation phase.
$NL 2
$C1 The translation phase using WORD mode.
At the beginning of the translation phase the following text is on the
screen: 
$UM
$RL 6
 .                                                                 .

   Tik hieronder het woord in dat vertaald moeten worden.

 .                                                                 .

$QM
The user can now enter the word he wants to be translated. 
If the input is ready the user must give the translation command. This can
be done by pressing the "TRANSLATE" function key.
$NL 1
The Rosetta3 system will now try to translate the input word.
Suppose the user has entered
the following input as response to the previous question:
$UM
$RL 7
 .                                                                 .

   Tik hieronder het woord in dat vertaald moeten worden.

   man 
 .                                                                 .
$QM
If the input word is ambiguous the system will
ask the user a question only in case the user has chosen the interactive mode.
Because "man" is ambiguous, the system will ask 
the following questions:
$UM
$RL 17
 .                                                                 .

   Wat is de betekenis van het woord "man" in de invoer regel:

   _man_ 


   U kunt een keuze maken uit de volgende alternatieven:

   0. geen keus

   1. persoon van het mannelijk geslacht
   2. echtgenoot

   Tik hier uw keuze in ( 0 - 2 ): 

 .                                                                 .
$QM
The user can now make a choice by typing the appropriate number followed
by a carriage return. If he chooses alternative "0" the system will
ultimately give all translations of the word "man". 
After the user has made his choice, e.g. "0", the system starts with the
translation. The translation results are presented as follows.
$UM
$RL 10
 .                                                                 .

   De Rosetta3 vertalingen zijn als volgt:

   _Nederlands_ :  man

   _Engels_     :  man                            


 .                                                                 .
$QM
In the first instance the Rosetta3 system gives only the best translation. If
the user also wants other translations he can get them by pressing function
key <NEXT> or <NEXT SENSE>. Pressing function key <NEXT> gives the next 
translation, whereas <NEXT SENSE> gives the best translation corresponding to
the next plausible meaning of the input word. In the above example the result
of pressing <NEXT> or <NEXT SENSE> will be the same, nl:
$UM
$RL 10
 .                                                                 .

   De Rosetta3 vertalingen zijn als volgt:

   _Nederlands_ :  man

   _Engels_     :  man                            
                 husband

 .                                                                 .
$QM
$NL
Furthermore the user can press after each translation one of the 
function keys <STOP>, <NEW> or <RESET>.
$NL
Pressing the <STOP> function key will result in termination of the
Rosetta3 session. Returning to the initial system state is possible by pressing
the <RESET> key. If the user wants to translate a new word he
must press the <NEW> function key. If the <NEW> function key is pressed the
following screen appears again:
$UM
$RL 6
 .                                                                 .

   Tik hieronder het woord in dat vertaald moeten worden.

 .                                                                 .

$QM
$NL
The user can now change the
system mode by pressing the <WORD> or <PHRASE> function key, but only _before_ 
he has pressed the <TRANSLATE> function key.  He can also
choose for the interactive system mode, using the <INT> key. However, the 
<INT> key is disabled in case the communication and source language is not 
Dutch.
$NL 1
If the user had entered a word which cannot be translated by the 
Rosetta3 system, e.g. "maz", the following message would have appeared on
the screen:
$UM
$RL 7
 .                                                                 .

   Rosetta3 kan het woord "maz" niet vertalen.

   
   Druk op <NEW> <STOP> of <RESET>
 .                                                                 .
$QM
The system will respond in the same way to these function keys as it did
in the previous example in which the input was correct.
$C1 The translation phase using PHRASE mode.
At the beginning of the translation phase the following text is on the
screen: 
$UM
$RL 6
 .                                                                 .

   Tik hieronder de frase in die vertaald moet worden.

 .                                                                 .

$QM
The user can now enter the words he wants to be translated. The words
have to be separated by spaces. The user can
start typing his words on a new line by entering a carriage return.
If the input is ready the user can give the translation command. This can
be done by pressing the "TRANSLATE" function key.
$NL 1
The Rosetta3 system will now try to translate the input phrase. 
Suppose the user has entered
the following input in response to the previous question:
$UM
$RL 6
 .                                                                 .

   Tik hieronder de frase in die vertaald moet worden.

   een man
 .                                                                 .
$QM
Then, in interactive mode, because both "een" and "man" are ambiguous, 
the system will ask the following questions:
$UM
$RL 18
 .                                                                 .

   Wat is de betekenis van het woord "een" in de invoer regel:

   _een_ man


   U kunt een keuze maken uit de volgende alternatieven:

   0. geen keus

   1. lidwoord
   2. telwoord


   Tik hier uw keuze in ( 0 - 2 ): 

 .                                                                 .
$QM
In the preceding example the system repeats the input line in which the
word in question occurs.  Likewise, the word is highlighted.
The user can now make a choice by typing in the appropriate number followed
by a carriage return. If he chooses alternative "0" the system will
ultimately give all translations of the word "man". Let us assume, however,
that the user type "1". Next the systems asks a question about "man":
$UM
$RL 18
 .                                                                 .

   Wat is de betekenis van het woord "man" in de invoer regel:

   een _man_


   U kunt een keuze maken uit de volgende alternatieven:

   0. geen keus

   1. persoon van het mannelijk geslacht
   2. echtgenoot

   Tik hier uw keuze in ( 0 - 2 ): 

 .                                                                 .
$QM
After the user has made his choice, e.g. "0", the system starts with the
translation. 
$NL
(NB. If a phrase contains an idiomatic expression, the system
asks the user whether he means the idiomatic or the normal reading of that
part of the phrase.)
$NL
The translation results are presented as follows, ( assuming
that the user typed the <NEXT> key once ).
$UM
$RL 10
 .                                                                 .

   De Rosetta3 vertalingen zijn als volgt:

   _Nederlands_ : een man

   _Engels_     : a man 
                a husband 

 .                                                                 .
$QM
Here the system behaves the same as it did in WORD mode at the same
point, that is, the function keys <NEW> <NEXT> <NEXT SENSE> <STOP> or <RESET>
can be pressed.
$NL
In case the Rosetta3 system cannot recognize one or more words of the
input sentence, e.g. if the user types "maz en vrow", it will respond
in _interactive_ mode with the following screen:
$UM
$RL 7
 .                                                                 .

   De onderstreepte woorden uit de invoer zijn Rosetta3 onbekend:

   _maz_ en _vrow_

 .                                                                 .
$QM
The user can now enter a new phrase using the <NEW> key.
However, if he wants the incorrect phrase to be translated he must
enter the <TRANSLATE> key. The translation results are then presented as
follows:
$UM
$RL 9
 .                                                                 .

   De Rosetta3 vertalingen zijn als volgt:

   _Nederlands_ : maz en vrow

   _Engels_     : <maz> and <vrow>

 .                                                                 .
$QM
These are the same results as the system would have produced in the
non-interactive mode.
