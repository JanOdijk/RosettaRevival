\section{Verbal flection}
\subsection{Introduction}
In chapter 1 an overview will be given of the preparatory studies which
led to the construction of the {\em Segmentation-rules} (i.e. Glue-rules
and Suffixtable) and {\em Lextree-rules} for the Spanish verbs.\\
In section 1.2 a first classification of Spanish verbs will be given, 
independent of the Rosetta framework. In section 1.3 the verbs will be 
organized in the light of their treatment in Rosetta. Finally, in section 
1.4 a global summary of the rules will be given.\\
The mentioned appendices can be found in document 438, which has not been
distributed but is kept in a ring binder.

\subsection{An inventory of Spanish verbs}
\subsubsection{Verbal characteristics}
First I made a provisional list of types of characteristics a Spanish
verb can have, providing each characteristic with a number:

\vspace{2ex}

\begin{tabular}{ll}
1)  &     o / ue (mover) \\
2)  &     e / ie (perder)\\
3)  &     e / i  (pedir) \\
3') &     irregular subj.pasado, derived from irregular pret\'{e}rito \\
    &     (condujieron $\rightarrow$ condujiera) \\
4)  &     c / g (decir) \\
5)  &     n / ng (poner) \\
6)  &     u / ue (jugar) \\
7)  &     i / ie (adquirir) \\
8)  &     l / lg (salir) \\
9)  &     s / sg (asir)  \\
10) &     o / u  (podrir)\\
10')&     o / u  only in gerundio  (poder) \\
11) &     g / gu (erguir)        (a general fonetic rule!!!) \\
12) &      -ig-  (caer) \\
13) &     "strong" pret\'{e}rito (decir) \\
14) &     futuro:  poder  $\rightarrow$  podr\'{e}. \\
15) &     futuro:  tener  $\rightarrow$  tendr\'{e}. \\
16) &     futuro:  querer $\rightarrow$  querr\'{e}. \\
16')&     "shortened" futuro: dir\'{e} ($<$decir) / har\'{e} ($<$hacer). \\
17) &     c / zc (conocer) \\
18) &     "strong" Ptc.pasado.  (abrir $\rightarrow$ abierto) \\
19) &     1st ps.sg. ending on -y (estoy, doy, voy) \\
20) &     short imperativo. (haz, di, ten, ven, pon, val)
\end{tabular}

\begin{tabular}{ll}
21) &     y / i  (y in presente. o\'{\i}r) \\
21')&     y / i  (no y in presente. caer)  \\
28) &     \'{\i}  (ampliar $\rightarrow$ ampl\'{\i}o; 
          efectuar $\rightarrow$  efect\'{u}o) \\
29) &     no accent (evacuar / apaciguar / abreviar) \\
    &     $\rightarrow$ verbs ending on -cuar/guar and some on -iar. \\
    &    (in fact this is the regular case!!! 28 is irregular) \\
30) &     reunir $\rightarrow$  re\'{u}no. \\
31) &     stem ending on \~{n} / ll: *ri\~{n}iendo $\rightarrow$ ri\~{n}endo. \\
32) &     h before ue (oler $\rightarrow$ huele) \\
33) &     re\'{\i}r (unstressed i of the V-ending disappears: 
          *rii\'{o} $\rightarrow$ ri\'{o}. \\
34) &     initial-i $\rightarrow$ y  (errar) \\
35) &     trema  (averiguar: averig\"{u}e) \\
36) &     cab $>$ quep (caber:  quepo, -a, subj./ind.presente) \\
37) &     ab  $>$ ep   (saber:  sepa, only subj.presente)\\
\end{tabular}

The following characteristics can in principal be considered {\em general 
phonetic rules}, meant to keep the correct (i.e. regular) pronounciation:
(N.B. But see section 1.4.2 on {\em Fonetic Information}).

\vspace{2ex}

\begin{tabular}{lll}
c - qu: &  c  / -- $\neg$e/i      &           (buscar  --  busqu\'{e}) \\
        &  qu / --  e/i                                       \\ \\

c - z:  &   z / -- $\neg$e/i      &           (cruzar  --  cruce) \\
        &   c / --  e/i      &           (vencer  --  venzo) \\ \\

g - j:  &   j / -- $\neg$e/i      &           (coger   --  cojo)  \\
        &   g / --  e/i                                      \\ \\

g - gu: &  g  / -- $\neg$e/i      &           (llegar   -- llegu\'{e}) \\
        &  gu / --  e/i      &           (distingo -- distinguir)
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Classification of verbs for the Rosetta framework}
\subsubsection{Stem and allomorphs}
Considering only the stem (i.e.not the V-endings) verbs can be roughly 
divided into two groups:
\begin{itemize}
\item Those verbs never having a stem change, e.g. hablar.
\item Those verbs having a stem change in certain forms, e.g. \\
                      despertar:  {\em despert}amos vs. {\em despiert}o
\end{itemize}
However, for several forms it has to be decided if it is irregular in
stem or irregular in ending. E.g. asgo (1st ps.sg.$<$ asir) can be split up
in two different ways: 
\begin{description}
\item  as-go (regular stem + irregular ending)
\item  asg-o (irregular stem + regular ending)
\end{description}
In this case the latter possibility has been chosen.\\ \\
Below follows a list of verbs plus their stem (i.e. the infinitive 
minus -ar/-er/-ir) and allomorph(s). Group zero contains verbs without
stem change (e.g. prender) as well as "real irregular verbs". \\
("@number" indicates the group of Chapter 1.2.2 to which the verb belongs.)\\
Verbs ending on -ar, -er and -ir have been split up into separate groups 
in this section.\\ \\
If a verb (often representing a class of verbs!) has an irregular form,
i.e. not composed of stem/allomorph + ending, the SFK for

\subsection{Simplification of rules}

The GLUE-rules for Spanish are divided into the following types:\\

\begin{tabular}{ll}
TYPE MGLUE2: & Middle GLUE-rules that divide a word into two segments.\\ \\
 \ \ \ e.g.: & de  + el :: del ; \\
             & con + m\'{\i} :: conmigo ; \\
             & and Irregular Imperativo-forms + one clitic:\\ 
             & pon + (A) :: pon(A) ; \\ \\

\subsubsection{Fonetic Information}
In March 1990 I added {\em Fonetic Information}, since in several cases no
general rules can be made. E.g. a rule is needed to change -c- into -qu-
in cases like: \\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ (Yo) indiqu\'{e} (indicar).\\

However, if this is stated as a general rule, the -c- will also change into
-qu- in cases like:\\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ que coque (cocer).\\

In other words, the change of consonants depends on the pronounciation of the
consonant in question and this, again, depends on the verbal ending.\\
E.g. colgar (g before a = like g in French 'garcon') versus coger (g before e =
like g in Dutch 'groot').\\ \\
I made suffix-rules with the following information:\\

\begin{tabular}{llll}
- *g   &  $\rightarrow$  &  FONgg  &  (e.g. colg-ar)\\
- *g   &  $\rightarrow$  &  FONch  &  (e.g. cog-er)\\ \\

- *c   &  $\rightarrow$  &  FONkk  &  (e.g. indic-ar)\\
- *c   &  $\rightarrow$  &  FONth  &  (e.g. coc-er)\\
\end{tabular}

\vspace{2ex}

In the dictionary, stems ending on -c and -g (except for the ones with
irrMedekCLASE) have been marked as follows:\\ \\
1)  $<$true,false,false,false$>$  (corresponding to FONgg)\\
2)  $<$false,true,false,false$>$  (corresponding to FONch)\\
3)  $<$false,false,true,false$>$  (corresponding to FONkk)\\
4)  $<$false,false,false,true$>$  (corresponding to FONth)\\



\subsubsection{Lextree-rules}

same attribute values as VERB, in order to make it possible to use the rules for
regular V-endings for both "{\bf stem} + regular ending" and

\vspace{2ex}

\begin{verbatim}
(1)   VERB    ===>   ALOVERB + SFCAT
       |                |
    SUBVERB          SUBVERB
       |                |
     BVERB            BVERB
\end{verbatim}

\begin{verbatim}                               |
(2)   VERB    ===>   SUBVERB   |(3) VERB   ===>   SUBVERB  +  SFCAT
       |                |      |      |               |
    SUBVERB           BVERB    |   SUBVERB          BVERB
       |                       |      |
     BVERB                     |    BVERB
\end{verbatim}

\begin{verbatim}
                               |
(4)  ALOVERB  ===>   SUBVERB   |(5)   ALOVERB   ===>  SUBVERB  +  SFCAT
        |               |      |         |               |
     SUBVERB          BVERB    |      SUBVERB          BVERB
        |                      |         |

\end{verbatim}

\begin{verbatim}
(6)  SUBVERB  ===>  BVERB

\section{Non-verbal flection}
\subsection{Introduction}
In this chapter an overview will be given of the {\em non-verbal}
flexional rules, i.e. rules for nouns, adjectives, adverbs, pronouns, 
determiners and cardinals.

\subsection{Segmentation-rules}
For the nouns, adjectives and pronouns the Segmentation-rules (in the
Suffixtable, Appendix A3) consist of the following rules:\\

\begin{itemize}
\item Rules for the irregular comparative forms, with SFKirrCompar, e.g. \\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ grande  +  SFKirrCompar  ::  mayor ;
\item Rules for the irregular plural of interrogative pronouns, e.g.\\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ qui\'{e}n   +  SFKirrPL      ::  qui\'{e}nes ;
\item Rules for the different number and gender of demonstrative adjectives and
  pronouns, e.g.\\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ ese     +  SFKmascDEMADJpl ::  esos ;

\begin{itemize}
\item Rules for pluralformation of both nouns and adjectives. \\
      ( SFKregPL, SFKirrPL, SFKextranPL )
\item Rules for ADJs which can have shortened forms, e.g. \\
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ bueno  +  SFKapocope  ::  bueno ;
\item Rules for the feminine forms of the ADJ, derived from the masculine form, 
with SFKregFEM.
\end{itemize}
     
\ \ NB. the \'{\i}simo-superlative and the mente-ADV are derivation, 
         rather than inflection. 

The following forms are derived from the subject
personal pronouns (which are: yo, t\'{u}, \'{e}l, ella, usted, nosotros, 
nosotras, vosotros, vosotras, ellos, allas, ustedes, ello):\\

\begin{tabular}{lll}
        yo  +  SFKcliticOD     & ::  me ;       &  (Direct Objects) \\     
        yo  +  SFKcliticIO     & ::  me ;       &  (Indirect Objects)  \\
        yo  +  SFKPronFuerte   & ::  m\'{\i} ;  &  (Strong Pronouns)    \\
        yo  +  SFKposAdj       & ::  mi ;       &  (Possessive Pronouns) \\
        yo  +  SFKposAdjPlur   & ::  mis ;      &  (Possessive Pronouns) \\
        yo  +  SFKposAdjFuerte & ::  m\'{\i}o ; &  (Strong Possessives) \\
\end{tabular}

\vspace{2ex}

Furthermore, there are rules to make the correct form (i.e. the correct
number and gender) of the following determiners:\\
ambos, varios, todo, mucho, uno, alguno and ninguno.\\ \\
Suffix-rules for Cardinals have been added recently by Elena
(these rules will be extended in the near future).\\ \\
Non-verbal Glue-rules are very limited, e.g. de + el $\rightarrow$ del \\
(see Appendices A1 and A2).

     \%cambioNOUNpl    & (with -s or -es, i.e. with a SFK)
\end{tabular}

\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Adjectives}
For adjectives the following rules are written (Appendix C2):
\begin{itemize}
  \item One rule to go from SUBADJ to BADJ. \\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \%SUBBASICadj\\

  \item Rules for the singular, which go from ADJ to SUBADJ (+ SFCAT):\\ 

\begin{tabular}{ll}
     \%ADJsgNOCAMBIO  &  (e.g. feliz, joven, comunista, etc.)               \\
     \%ADJsgCAMBIOfem &  (e.g. guapo $\rightarrow$ guapa / holgaz\'{a}n     \\
                      &   $\rightarrow$ holgazana)                          \\
     \%ADJsgCAMBIOmasc&  (the default form in the lexicon).                 \\
     \%Ap\'{o}copeADJ &  (ADJs with shortened forms; it is decided in the   \\
                      &   syntax in which cases the shortened form is used.)\\
                      &  (There is one plural-case: cien(to).)\\
\end{tabular}

  \item One rule for the plural, from ADJ to ADJ + SFCAT:\\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \%ADJpl \ \ \ \ \ (for forms without ap\'{o}cope.)\\

  \item Two rules for demonstrative adjectives (ese, este, aquel), 
        from DEMADJ to BDEMADJ (+ SFCAT):\\ \\
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \%DemAdj \\
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \%DemAdjMascSing\\

\item One rule for Interrogative adjectives, from WHADJ to BWHADJ + SFCAT:\\

\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \%WhAdj \\

\item There is also one rule for irregular comparatives, which doesn't have 
      a name yet.

\end{itemize}


\subsubsection{Adverbs}
For Adverbs three lextree-rules exist (Appendix C3):\\
\begin{itemize}
\item  \%ADVSUBadv  (ADJ $\rightarrow$ SUBADV).
\item  \%SUBBASICadv
\item  \%AdjtoAdvDerivMENTE (SUBADV $\rightarrow$ ADJ + SFCAT).
\end{itemize}


\subsubsection{Pronouns}
The following Lextree-rules are written for the personal pronouns 
(Appendix C4):\\

\begin{tabular}{ll}
     \%DOclitics      &   direct object clitics.                   \\
     \%IOclitics      &   indirect object clitics.                 \\
     \%PrepPronFuerte &   strong pronouns, after a preposition.    \\
     \%PronNomFuerte  &   strong pronouns, default form "nomfuerte".\\
     \%PronPrepFuerte &   strong pronouns, default form "prepfuerte".\\
     \%posADJsg       &   possessive adjectives, singular.         \\
     \%posADJpl       &   possessive adjectives, plural.           \\
     \%posPROsg       &   possessive pronouns, singular.           \\
     \%posPROsgFEM    &   possessive pronouns, singular feminine.  \\
     \%posPROplur     &   possessive pronouns, plural.             \\
     \%WHPROtoBWHPRO  &   Interrogative pronouns.
\end{tabular}       


\subsubsection{Determiners}

For determiners two rules have been written (Appendix C5): \\ \\
\%DETflection  (for determiners with flection, e.g. todo $\rightarrow$ todas.)\\
\%DETnoflection (for determiners without flection, e.g. cada.)\\


\subsubsection{Cardinals}

For cardinals the following two rules exist (Appendix C6): \\ \\
\%CARDrule1 (for one digit).\\
\%CARDrule2 (for all cardinals).
