\section{Introduction}

This document gives a fairly extended list of Spanish verbpatterns and it 
supplies some additional information on them. It is based on the Dutch 
verbpattern list created by Jan Odijk and it has been discussed with him as 
well. This document
must be seen as a document of work in progress. It can function as a 
preliminary document for reference. 

The verbpatterns treated are ordered alphabetically.

For each verbpattern the following additional information is supplied:

\begin{description}
  \item[Rule] The name of the M-rule treating this verbpattern.
  \item[Canonical Surface Representations] An indication of how this 
verbpattern is realized in structures that are the output of M-generator.
(Hence an indication of what kind of structures the surface parser must be
able to deal with). {\em  Canonical} is supposed to mean here that no
special rules such as {\em  shift rules} etc. have applied. In certain cases
the specification {\em  \VR} has been given, meaning that the canonical
surface representations must be compatible with the form of 
Verb Raising structures.
  \item[Remarks]    Any remarks that seem appropriate. 
\end{description}

Furthermore for each {\em  thetavp} compatible with the verbpattern
being treated {\bf example verbs} and {\bf example sentences} have been
supplied in special subsections.


An index of all example verbs is added.

NB. For more examples see doc.301 (Spanish M-rules: VERBPPROPformation), the
section on Verbpatterns.
