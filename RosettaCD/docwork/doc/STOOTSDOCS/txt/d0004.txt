_8. Rosetta3_

In the course of this year the external specifications of Rosetta3 must be
given. Established facts are:
- it will be a phrase translation system Dutch-English and English-Dutch
- it will be based on the Van Dale dictionaries for foreign languages
- a generation component for Spanish on the basis of a very modest dictionary
  will be made as an exercise
- w.r.t. the scope it must cover at least the translation of
  . stems of words
  . conjugated, inflected or declined words
  . idiomatic expressions
  . short sentences
The following issues w.r.t. Rosetta3 were raised in a brain storm session:
- Starting point for Rosetta3 is that the user must know the target language
  and that Rosetta3 will only be a help. At most Rosetta3 will order the
  possible translations according to plausibility.
- Limitations caused by the Van Dale dictionaries:
  . they are large dictionaries with only little syntactic and morphological
    information, so that adding information will be a lot of work.
  . description of the meaning is only available in Dutch.
- Rosetta3 may be should have the performance of a conventional dictionary, but
  that will not be of our main concern for some time to come.
- Userfriendliness
  . the user should be able to make choices for modes in a menu driven way; he
    should, for example, have the possibility to indicate that he only wants
    the translation of a (some kind of) word, (some kind of) phrase or (some
    kind of) clause. This could link up with ideas about grammars for parts of
    sentences or phrases and robustness.
  . there should be a facility to choose between a one or more translations
    output
  . it should be possible to have up and down scrolling
- Interpretation and the user
  . the user may want a description of the meaning (only possible for Dutch
    and may be only feasible for words)
  . the only neat way to give an interpretation seems to be an interactive 
    dialogue with the user. Should there be interaction in Rosetta3?
- Activities that will be of some use in Rosetta4 must be preferred: that means
  in any case an as rich as possible syntax and morphology.
- Scope of Rosetta3: a minimum seems to be declarative main clauses.
- Explanation of the scope and performance to the user:
  . size: it should not fill more than one page
  . it should link up with the knowledge of grammar etc. of the average user
    (problem: what is that?)
  . it must be possible to explain that only simple declarative sentences and
    not complex sentences can be translated
  . a user also can learn about the system by using it.
- What is the translation of conjugated forms? Some proposals:
  . the bare infinitive
  . the infinitive + the information that it is a conjugated form
  . the infinitive + information about the kind of conjugated form
  . translation with help of interaction
  May be we should make a difference between autonomous translations and those
  that are induced by the grammar.
- The time performance of the system should be improved.
- A batch mode facility would be nice for testing purposes.
_Conclusion_: somebody should write a proposal about this subject and then we
            will talk about it again.
