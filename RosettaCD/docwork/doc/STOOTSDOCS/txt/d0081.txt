$C1 Introduction
$nl 1
$ This document gives a listing of the syntactic constructions of Dutch,
English and Spanish. The document consists of a number of sections, each
dealing with the phrases one can form with one 'lexical category' (VERB, NOUN,
PREP, ADJ and ADV, respectively) as its head, followed by a section on
Coordination. Every section is divided into three subsection, one for each
relevant language (Dutch, English, Spanish). Every section gives a listing of
possible construction types having the relevant lexical category as its head.
Every construction type has been assigned a unique number. This numbering is
systematic to a certain extent, as indicated in each subsection. One of the
functions of this numbering is to facilitate the mapping of the 
constructions of the different languages upon each other. (NB. It 
is to be expected
that this mapping will only partly amount to the linking of 
constructions with identical 
numbers.)$

$ The listing of the Spanish constructions is not meant to be exhaustive. Only
those constructions are taken into consideration that are necessary for Spanish
to function as an adequate target language.
In the sections on Spanish the construction types that exist in Dutch (or
(English) but that do not belong to the spanish grammar, are marked with **.
For such cases a translation is added (between braces).
The alternative constructions used in the translation is always listed 
independently as well (under
the appropiate construction type number).$

$ Every construction
has been assigned a priority, in the following way:$ 
$nl 1
+ : Rosetta3 will be able to handle this construction.
0 : Rosetta3 will be able to handle this construction if the relevant 
    construction can be dealt with without requiring special efforts.
- : Rosetta3 will not be able to handle this construction, unless it
    requires special effort to exclude it.

$ The priority rating has been assigned on the basis of a rough estimation of
the frequency of its occurrence in informative texts, and on the basis of an
estimation of the degree of difficulty of analyzing the relevant constructions
adequately. Translational problems to be expected did not affect the
priority rating.$

$ The numbering of the NP-constructions is of the following format:$

2.H.T._C.d_/_d_

$ - where H can take on the following values:$

$ H = 1..5, as indicated under the heading 'Waarden van N'; The letter _x_ 
serves as a variable for these values if the actual values are irrelevant.$
$ H = M    Gemarkeerd$
$nl 1
$ - where T can take on the following values:$
$nl 1
$ T = 1  Specifier$
$ T = 2  Complement$
$ T = 3  Modifier$
$nl 1
$ The intended nature of the the distinction between these three classes is 
as follows. Complements of a nominal projection belong to the subcategorized
adjuncts of a noun. They tend to to occur in postnominal positions.
 Specifiers are instantiations of an optional,
non-recursive category. They tend to to occur in prenominal positions.
Modifiers constitue a recursive categorythat shows up 
both in postnominal as in prenominal position. $

$ - where C is a category name or an individual word, and _d_ can be any digit.$

$ In a few cases the numbering is exceptional, namely if an adjunct is not
attached to a non-maximal projection of N, but to a phrase as a whole. For
example in case of a 'bijstelling'.$

$ The distinction between modifiers and specifiers as made in the section on
NPs does not apply to this section in a straightforward way. Unlike
NP-modifiers and NP-specifiers, the non-heads listed under 4.3 (5.3) and 4.2
(4.2) are in complementary distribution. However, the 'maatconstituenten' under

$c2 Adverbial phrases

$ Adverbials are a fairly heterogeneous class. The construction types 
listed in this subsection are not posible for all subclasses of adverbials.
The adverbials that can be attached to the largest number 
of string types are those derived from adjectives.
In this section the distinction between modifiers and specifiers is 
as problematic as in the previous section on adjectival phrases.$

$ Coordinations of the major categories (NP, S, VP, AdjP, AdvP, PP) have
been given, nonelliptic and elliptic. In certain cases coordinations of
nodes within these major categories have been added (e.g. coordination within
NP's (of Nbar's?). Finally, so-called Right Node Raising constructions have
been mentioned.$
